Nintendo

What is Nintendo

is a Japanese multinational video game company headquartered in Kyoto,Japan. It develops video games and video game consoles. Nintendo was founded in 1889 as Nintendo Karuta by craftsman Fusajiro Yamauchi and originally produced handmade hanafuda playing cards. After venturing into various lines of business during the 1960s and acquiring a legal status as a public company, Nintendo distributed its first console, the Color TV-Game, in 1977. It gained international recognition with the release of Donkey Kong in 1981 and the Nintendo Entertainment System and Super Mario Bros. in 1985.

Game Boy

was the first handheld game console sold by Nintendo that featured interchangeable ROM cartridges for each game, unlike the Game & Watch that had a different system for each game. (1989)

Super Nintendo Entertainment System

is a 16-bit video game console released by Nintendo in North America, South America, Europe, Asia, Oceania and Africa. (1990)

Nintendo 64

commonly called the N64, and codenamed Ultra 64, was Nintendo's third home video game console for the international market. It was released with three launch games in Japan and two in North America. (1996)

Nintendo GameCube

It was Nintendo's sixth generation game console, the same generation as Sega's Dreamcast, Sony's PlayStation 2, and Microsoft's Xbox. (2001)

Wii

was released on November 19, 2006 as Nintendo's seventh-generation home console. Nintendo designed the console to appeal towards a wider audience than those of its main competitors, ps3 and xbox360 (2006)

Wii U

was released on November 18, 2012 as a direct successor to the Wii, and the first entry in the eighth generation of home video game consoles. (2012)

Nintendo Switch

was released on March 3, 2017, and is Nintendo's second entry in the eighth generation of home video game consoles. (2017)
